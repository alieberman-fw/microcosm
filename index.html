Why now

01

The science landed

Generative-agent research proved LLM populations exhibit believable emergent social behavior — memory, reflection, and social influence included. By 2024, interview-grounded agents replicated real individuals' survey responses at 85% of their own test-retest consistency.

02

The economics flipped

Frontier-model inference costs have collapsed by orders of magnitude. A 5,000-agent submarket deliberation now costs less than recruiting a single human respondent — and resolves before the coffee is cold.

30–90 SECONDS PER RUN
03

The ground truth exists

Census, ACS, MLS, migration, and lending data are digitized down to the submarket. For the first time, a synthetic population can be seeded to match a real place — and scored against what that place actually did.

SEEDED, THEN BACKTESTED
A grounded swarm is a leading indicator.

The market reveals demand 12–24 months after capital is committed. A simulated population — grounded in a submarket's actual demographics, transactions, and migration — reveals it before. What to build, where, for whom, at what price: the hardest questions in real estate stop being answered in hindsight.

Validation

Calibration is the product.

Synthetic populations are directional, not oracular — and we say so. Every Microcosm prediction is logged, scored against realized outcomes, and labeled with a reliability class. The result is something no point estimate can offer: a live, auditable track record.

The research anchor: interview-grounded generative agents replicated survey responses at 85% of humans' own two-week test-retest consistency — with reduced demographic bias (Stanford, n=1,052).
